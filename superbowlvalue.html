The Value

Viewers

Although the viewing of the Super Bowl fluctuates throughout the years, the number of viewers usually lingers around 100 Million. This makes it the most watched thing on television every year (In the USA). The publicity that the Super Bowl attracts is absurd, which makes the amount of money that it takes in also enormous.

Ticket Prices

The amount of money it costs to purchase a ticket might just be a literal arm and a leg. The next Super Bowl, Super Bowl LIII, has its tickets starting at $5000. That is an insane price, considering that the tickets for a normal, regular season game are around $150. These Super Bowl tickets don't last long either. They are bought by resellers and sold for inhumane prices. A good explanation for the inflated price of these tickets is that only a fraction of people who go are not in the 1%. Most of the people that attend Super Bowls are in large companies that go and advertise. Also, you will find many celebrities there, too.

Commercials

Commercials are the reason that so many people watch the Super Bowl. A good portion of the 100 million who are tuning in are only there for the commercials, and in reality they probably do not even understand the sport. Companies like Coca-Cola, Pepsi, GoDaddy, and MnMs make frequent appearances in the Big Game to attract customers. The average price to have a commercial play during the Big Game is around four and a half million dollars for just a 30-second showing. The price of these commercials not only gives the NFL loads of money, but is probably one of the most high-risk high-reward decisions a company can make.

Halftime Show

The Super Bowl Halftime Show is another huge reason why the event gets so many views on television. Basically, every Super Bowl has a performer that it appointed to it. The main performer may also have some side performers that feature. The Halftime Show is like an elaborate, 30-minute concert during halftime. Artists who have run the show include Beyonce, Bruno Mars, Katy Perry, Lady Gaga, Michael Jackson, and many more.
